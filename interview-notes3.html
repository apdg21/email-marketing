🧪 testing pitfalls · klaviyo (and lessons for OmniSend)

“I signed up, browsed, added to cart… but nothing fired.” — common fix checklist

🍪 1. identification gap

Klaviyo needs to “cookie” you. Use ?utm_email=you@example.com in URL to force identify.

🧩 2. missing snippet

“Added to Cart” often needs manual JS in theme.liquid. If theme changed, code may be lost. OmniSend uses app embed – more automatic.

📉 3. metric never fired

Klaviyo won't show trigger in builder until event happened once. Check Analytics > Metrics after forcing event.

✅ safe test checklist:

  • Watch “Active on Site” real‑time feed – if you see yourself, master tracking works.
  • Incognito kills cookies → use normal window + fresh email.
  • Turn off “Smart Sending” for tests (prevents blocking due to recent sends).

🎭 “ugly but high‑revenue” flow dilemma

If it ain't broke, don't just fix it — A/B test it. revenue is king, but brand consistency matters.

⚖️ risk vs reward

Never replace a winner – A/B test. Keep “ugly/good” as Control (A) vs “clean/new” as Challenger (B) in OmniSend.

🔄 when to update despite revenue

Brand redesign, technical decay (old HTML breaks), or high unsubscribe rate (>5%) even if revenue is good.

🔁 what to change first (hierarchy of impact):

1. Offer (biggest revenue impact) 2. Subject line (opens) 3. Layout/visuals (CTR) 4. Copy (vibe & brand)

🗣️ interview answer snippet:

“If a flow performs well despite poor structure, I wouldn’t overhaul it. I’d A/B test a cleaner version against it – keep timing/offer, test layout. I protect revenue while improving brand image.”

🔄 your flywheel effect

Trust → retention (low unsub) → list growth → more flow triggers → more revenue.

“I use campaigns to build trust & test new layouts; flows are the foundation I don’t recklessly touch.”

📈 metric to watch:

List growth rate vs unsubscribe rate. Healthy ratio = flows scale.

🏛️ 1. Stability

Don't touch high‑revenue flows without reason. No reckless changes with client money.

🤝 2. Trust

Campaigns build relationship (not just sell). Increases LTV and lowers unsubscribes.

📈 3. Growth

More subscribers = more flow revenue. Flywheel effect.

🧠 mental block? remember the 3 pillars: Stability Trust Growth + “ugly but profitable → A/B test”
⚡ pro tip: when interviewer asks about flow edits, reference “flywheel + defensive optimisation”
