Email Marketing Client Onboarding Playbook

A complete, practical playbook for onboarding new email marketing clients

Ordered, action-oriented, and covers everything from tech checks to flows, campaigns, QA and analysis.

Quick Orientation

This guide provides a runnable sequence of tasks with concrete checks, flow templates, campaign build & QA checklists, reporting KPIs, and deliverables to hand to the client.

Use it as a checklist you can tick off in one onboarding session.

Phase 1 ‚Äî Immediate Access & Discovery

1

Get Access (Credentials & Roles)

  • Admin access to email platform (Klaviyo/Mailchimp/Omnisend/HubSpot/etc.)
  • Access to store platform (Shopify, WooCommerce, etc.) with admin/dev permissions
  • DNS access or contact for DNS manager
  • Access to analytics (Google Analytics/GA4), payment provider, and any CRM
  • Slack/Email/Asana project contact for dev & marketing approvals

Discovery Document

  • Business model, primary revenue drivers, average order value, seasonality
  • Current email goals (revenue, retention, LTV, acquisition, deliverability)
  • Current sending identity: sending domains, IPs, from name(s), reply-to
  • Compliance rules & regional regulations (GDPR, CAN-SPAM, PECR, CASL)
  • Existing templates, brand assets, product feed, promo calendar

Phase 2 ‚Äî Tech & Deliverability Audit

2

Run these checks immediately and record findings before sending major campaigns.

Domain & Authentication

  • Check SPF, DKIM, DMARC presence and correctness
  • Confirm mail.from and return-path alignment if using a subdomain

Sending Infrastructure

  • Check if account is on shared or dedicated IP
  • If dedicated, verify it's warmed; if shared, check reputation
  • Prepare warm-up plan for new domains

Inbox Placement / Seed Testing

  • Create seed list with test addresses across providers
  • Send tests and check inbox vs spam placement
  • Test email client previews (Gmail, Outlook, Apple Mail, etc.)

Suppression List & Bounce Handling

  • Export and inspect suppression & bounce lists
  • Confirm unsubscribe behavior and suppression logic

Data & Event Integrity

  • Verify e-commerce events are tracked: Placed Order, Started Checkout, Added to Cart, etc.
  • Check customer profile properties are mapped correctly
  • Verify timestamp/timezone consistency

Privacy & Consent

  • Confirm opt-in sources and single/double opt-in usage
  • Verify marketing consent flags are stored (GDPR compliance)
  • Ensure forms pass consent data into profiles

Feed & Product Catalog

  • Ensure product feed includes SKU, price, product URL, main image, inventory status

Phase 3 ‚Äî Data Hygiene & Segmentation Checks

3

List Health

  • Remove known role addresses and low-quality addresses
  • Identify duplicates and malformed emails
  • Run email validation check with optional third-party tool

Segmentation Baseline

Create these baseline segments:

  • Active buyers (last 90 days)
  • One-time buyers
  • High LTV / VIP customers
  • Email subscribers without purchase
  • Cart abandoners (24-72h)
  • Dormant customers (last purchase > 12 months)

Ensure each segment updates dynamically and is available for flows/campaign targeting.

Custom Fields

  • Identify must-have custom properties (size, gender_preference, birthday, etc.)
  • Ensure fallback values are set for personalization tokens

Phase 4 ‚Äî Flow & Trigger Audit

4

Walk through every automated flow and validate triggers, timing, content, and metrics.

Essential Flows to Check / Create

  • Welcome series (trigger: subscribe)
  • Browse abandonment (trigger: viewed product but no add to cart)
  • Cart abandonment (trigger: added to cart but no purchase)
  • Checkout reminder (started checkout)
  • Post-purchase sequences
  • Win-back (trigger: no purchase in X days)
  • VIP & loyalty programs
  • Birthday / anniversary campaigns
  • Failed payment / subscription renewal reminders
  • Suppression / re-engagement flows

For Each Flow Check

  • Trigger event name and conditions are firing correctly
  • Delay timings and exit conditions are appropriate
  • Dynamic content rendered correctly
  • Unsubscribe & preference center links present
  • Flow metrics: open rate, click rate, conversion rate, revenue per recipient

Phase 5 ‚Äî Campaign Planning, Data Review & Strategy Development

5

Step 1: Historical Data Review (at least 3 months)

Before planning anything new, analyze the past 3 months of campaigns to understand what worked and what didn't.

Key items to check:

  • Performance trends: open rate, click rate, conversion rate, revenue per recipient
  • Top performers: highest revenue campaigns, best subject lines
  • Underperformers: identify causes (targeting, timing, content, technical issues)
  • Segment engagement patterns
  • Deliverability and engagement decay over time
  • Content mapping of recent campaigns

Step 2: Strategy Planning ‚Äî Continuity Over Restart

Use findings to shape a continuity strategy that maintains audience trust and performance momentum.

Step 3: Build 3 Strategic Sets for the Next 3-6 Months

Strategy Set 1: Retention & Relationship

Focus: Strengthen engagement with existing customers and loyal subscribers.

Tactics: Educational content, VIP rewards, personalized recommendations, UGC.

Example campaigns: "You'll love this ‚Äî our most-loved product this month", "VIP early access to our fall collection".

Strategy Set 2: Conversion & Revenue Growth

Focus: Drive direct sales and maximize AOV and frequency.

Tactics: Promotion cadence, retargeting abandoners, bundles/upsells, urgency tactics.

Example campaigns: "Only 24 hours left ‚Äî your favorites are almost gone", "Buy 2, get 1 free ‚Äî this week only".

Strategy Set 3: Re-engagement & List Health

Focus: Reconnect with inactive subscribers and maintain strong deliverability.

Tactics: Win-back campaigns, preference updates, gradual frequency reduction.

Example campaigns: "Still want to hear from us? Update your preferences.", "We saved you a spot ‚Äî come back for something special."

Step 4: Implementation Plan

Map the next 8-12 weeks of campaigns across the 3 strategy sets.

Recommended balance: 60% Retention & Relationship, 30% Conversion & Revenue, 10% Re-engagement

Step 5: Build, QA & Launch

Follow technical and creative checklist:

  • UTM & naming conventions
  • Template modularity
  • Proofing & QA
  • Send time optimization
  • Monitor live results within the first hour

Step 6: Post-campaign Learnings

After each send, compare results to historical averages and strategy set benchmarks. Document insights in a performance tracker.

Phase 6 ‚Äî Post-Send Monitoring & Reporting

6

Immediate Checks (within 1-2 hours)

  • Send rate & velocity
  • Hard bounces spike
  • Spam complaints (keep <0.1% ideally)
  • Unsubscribe rate (contextual ‚Äî abnormally high is a red flag)

24-72 Hour Analysis

Key metrics to report:

  • Delivered % (sends ‚àí bounces)
  • Open rate, Click rate, Click-to-open rate (CTO)
  • Conversion rate (orders from email clicks)
  • Revenue / ORD (revenue per delivered), Revenue per recipient (RPR)
  • Average order value from campaign
  • Unsubscribe & complaint rates
  • Top clicked links and best performing creative

Flow Performance

For each automated flow track: opens, clicks, purchase rate, LTV of flowed customers, revenue per email.

Weekly & Monthly Dashboards

Aggregate channel KPIs, flows performance, list growth, engagement cohorts.

Phase 7 ‚Äî Optimization Playbook

7

A/B Testing

Typical A/B candidates: subject line, from name, preheader, hero image, CTA copy, one vs multiple products, discount vs no discount.

Segmentation Experiments

Test targeted creative to smaller, high-value segments vs. broad list to measure uplift.

Frequency & Cadence

Monitor unsubscribes & complaints by frequency; adjust cadence for different segments.

Flow Optimizations

If cart flow has low conversion: test timings, add product image & social proof, experiment with discount in final touch.

Deliverability Maintenance

Clean list quarterly: remove consistently unengaged after multi-stage win-back.
